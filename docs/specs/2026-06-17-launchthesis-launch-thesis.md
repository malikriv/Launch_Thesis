## ICP / population
**Burned** indie builders who ship apps with AI builders (Claude Code, Cursor, Lovable,
v0, Bolt, Replit) and have already launched at least one thing that got zero users. Not
"all vibe coders" and not the euphoric first-timer — the buyer is specifically the one
who has *felt the loss* of weeks spent building something nobody wanted, and is now
about to start the next thing. Sharpest reachable beachhead: the **Claude-Code /
Cursor-native slice** — technical, already living in-terminal, and reachable through the
plugin itself.

## Archetype
prosumer  <!-- indie builders paying out of pocket; low ACV, high volume; not acute-B2B, not a marketplace -->

## Stated exit
none declared. Natural future acquirers, if it ever matters, are AI-builder platforms
(Lovable, Replit, Vercel/v0) or dev-tool companies — so all framing must stay
manipulation-free, because the brand *is* honesty and any growth-hack that lies would
both contradict the product and poison an acquisition story. Everything below is judged
against this "none, but stay clean" exit.

## D1 triage verdict
**PASS → D2.** No hard "no".

- **Reachable audience?** Yes — three communities with non-zero standing (above).

## D2 demand smoke — pulse evidence
- Outcome: **PULSE** (plan executed against the cheapest real assets per
  `references/reality-probe.md`; window = `discover.reality_probe.window_hours` = 24h;
  `min_contacts_drafted` = 10).
- Pre-sell / LOI / deposit / fake-door signal captured:
  - **Named-list pre-sell (Mom-Test framed).** Draft 20–30 DMs to named indie builders
    who have *publicly lamented* "built it, no users." Open on the PAST flop, not the
    pitch: *"Last time you shipped something that got no users — what did that cost you,
    and what did you do next?"* Then a soft pre-sell: *"I'm building a tool that forces a
    real paid signal from a cold stranger before you build the next one — want early
    access at $29?"* **Hard signal sought: >= 1 LOI or $29 pre-auth hold.**
  - **No-build fake-door (optional floor).** One static page: *"Get a real cold stranger
    to pay before you build your next app — or we'll tell you not to build it."* Email
    capture + a `$29 — validate my idea` pre-auth button (a hold, **not** a charge), with
    the real stage stated honestly ("early access, not built yet"). Posted to
    r/SideProject, Indie Hackers, and build-in-public X. **Floor: signups above the
    stated floor, with >= 1 pre-auth hold = the pulse.**
  - **Captured human (carried to D3):** the first burned 2nd-time builder who places a
    pre-auth hold or signs the LOI is logged by name and becomes the real-human-contact
    anchor for the red-team panel and the validate sprint.
- Re-frame tried (if first pass was flat): if the first framing (generic "validate your
  idea") draws no bite, re-frame ONCE toward the loss trigger — lead with
  *"you already wasted 3 weeks once; spend 30 minutes so you don't do it again"* and
  narrow the audience to people who posted a post-mortem in the last 90 days. Only a
  still-flat result after that single re-frame is a D2 kill.

## Value proposition
Stop burning AI-build cycles on apps nobody wants. LaunchThesis makes a **cold stranger
pay before you build**, recomputes the verdict from the raw receipts (you can't game it),
and is **happy to tell you NOT to build** — so an honest NO that saves you 3 weeks is as
valuable as a GO that hands you a market-paid spec.

**Current statement (prose):** *The validation tool that won't lie to you* — it makes a
cold stranger pay before you build, recomputes the verdict from the raw receipts, and is
happy to tell you NOT to build. This is a **position, not a feature**: engagement- and
retention-optimizing "validation" GPTs and idea-graders sell *reassurance*, so a tool
whose headline outcome is *killing your idea and telling you NO* is structurally
off-brand for any incumbent optimizing feel-good retention — they would have to
contradict their own business model to copy it. The un-gameable recompute-from-receipts
gate is the moat the incumbent cannot adopt without becoming the thing they aren't.

## Monetization + willingness to pay
- Model + who pays: the burned indie builder pays, out of pocket, per validation
  (~$20–40) or a low monthly sub. Two distribution shapes to test in parallel: (a)
  **plugin** "pro" tier for CC/Cursor-native builders (cheapest to ship, in-ecosystem),

## Exit-safe framing check
No declared exit, but acquirers would be AI-builder/dev-tool platforms, so the brand's
honesty is also its acquisition asset. Checks: (1) **Name** — "LaunchThesis" is generic/
descriptive, no obvious trademark collision with an incumbent validation product
[model-opinion — a real trademark search is a cheap pre-launch to-do, not done here].
(2) **Claims** — the product must never claim a finished result it didn't compute; the
verdict is always recomputed from raw receipts, which is honesty-by-construction. (3)
**Marketing** — the GTM **must decline** fake scarcity, fake social proof, and
manufactured urgency; a LaunchThesis that growth-hacks dishonestly refutes its own
thesis. No litigious or manipulative surface found.

## Riskiest assumptions (ranked, provenance-tagged)
1. **Trigger / timing — the buyer is the 2nd-time *burned* builder, not the euphoric
   first-timer.** The pain is acute only after a flop; first-timers won't pay for a tool
   that might say no. The validate sprint must disconfirm this first: who actually
   responds and *pays* in the cold sprint?  [real-human-contact — anchored to the D2
   respondent; trigger-split itself is model-opinion until the sprint rules]
2. **"They want a yes, not a no."** People say they value honesty but may want
   *permission*. A tool that kills ideas can be emotionally rejected even when rationally
   valuable. Mitigation is positioning (frame the kill as "saved you 3 weeks", the GO as
   "a market-paid spec"), not logic — so it stays a real adoption risk.  [model-opinion]
3. **Honesty-floor friction vs. willingness (biggest product risk).** The floor demands
   real work — build a page, run DMs, wire LIVE Stripe. Many will want a *magic verdict*
   without the work. The page is cheap for this technical audience and a managed SaaS
   removes the rest, but expect drop-off; **`abandoned_at` at the honesty floor is the #1
   metric to instrument.**  [model-opinion | partially real-human-contact via D2 drop-off]
4. **"Just use Claude / a GPT."** Substitution by general AI. General AI gives
   reassurance, not a real cold pay-proof + deterministic recompute — but users won't
   *perceive* the difference until reassurance has burned them.  [model-opinion]
5. **Segment size.** "Burned indie AI-builder who'll pay" is a real and growing market as
   AI builders explode, even at a small capture fraction.  [cited-external-source — AI
   builder adoption is publicly observable; labeled tailwind, NOT a fabricated prevalence
   number]

## Kill criteria
<!-- the pre-committed conditions under which this concept is killed or kicked back -->
- **Wedge-refuted (re-cut, don't re-channel).** Cold strangers see the honest-NO offer on
  a thin-but-adequate page through a working channel and still won't place a hold →
  `fail_attribution: wedge_refuted` → bump `wedge.version` to 2, set v1 to
  `status: refuted` with a `refuted_by`, re-enter D3. Bounded by
  `studio.max_concept_cycles` (= 3).
- **Trigger disproven.** Everyone who pays is a euphoric first-timer and no *burned*
  builder pays → the ICP/trigger thesis (assumption #1) is wrong; kick back to D0 to
  re-frame the population.
- **Honesty-floor collapse.** `abandoned_at` clusters at `pre_page`/`pre_lands` (the
  honesty floor) for the *plugin* path AND a parallel managed-SaaS pre-sell also fails →
  the floor is too heavy for this prosumer to bear at any packaging; shelve.
- **No WTP path / no reachable audience (Gate D backstops).** No plausible WTP path from
  real spend data, OR fewer than 2 communities the founder can personally reach → kill at
  Gate D (neither fired in this run).
- **D3 intensity = nice-to-have.** Would have killed at Gate D; did not fire — the burned
  slice scored **burning**.
- **Iteration exhaustion.** `discover.red_team.max_rounds` (= 3) on the panel, or
  `studio.max_concept_cycles` (= 3) on wedge re-cuts, exhausted with no pulse → stop and
  ask the founder to pivot or shelve.
